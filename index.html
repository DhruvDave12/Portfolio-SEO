Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the Thiel Fellowship?

The Thiel Fellowship is a two-year program that awards $200,000 to young people who want to build new things and create impact, rather than pursuing traditional classroom education. It supports aspiring entrepreneurs by providing capital, mentorship, and network access to launch and grow their ventures.

Who is eligible for the Thiel Fellowship?

The fellowship is primarily for young individuals, typically under the age of 23, who possess exceptional drive, creativity, and a compelling vision for a project or company. Applicants are selected based on their potential for impact and innovation, not academic credentials. Detailed eligibility criteria can be found on our Eligibility & Application page.

What kind of projects does the fellowship support?

The Thiel Fellowship embraces a broad spectrum of innovative projects across diverse sectors including technology, science, engineering, social impact, arts, and more. The common thread is a focus on building something novel and impactful that challenges existing paradigms. We fund disruptive innovation grants for youth.

What happens after the two-year program?

Upon completion, fellows join a powerful global alumni network. Many continue to develop their ventures, successfully raise further funding, or pursue other entrepreneurial endeavors. The fellowship provides a robust foundation for long-term career success and sustained innovation.

How do I apply for the Thiel Fellowship?

The application process involves submitting an online application, essays detailing your project and vision, and multiple rounds of interviews. Specific deadlines and requirements are announced annually. We encourage interested candidates to review our comprehensive Application Process page for full details.

Is college still an option after the fellowship?

While the fellowship encourages fellows to defer or opt out of traditional college education to focus fully on their projects, individuals are free to choose their own path post-fellowship. Many continue their entrepreneurial journey, while some may decide to return to academia or join other organizations.
