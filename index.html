About the Thiel Fellowship: $200K Grant for Young Innovators

The Thiel Fellowship is a two-year program for young people who want to build new things instead of going to college. Founded by Peter Thiel, the fellowship offers a $200,000 grant and a powerful network of mentors, investors, and founders to help fellows bring their most ambitious projects to life.

We believe that true innovation often happens outside the traditional academic system. Our program is designed for aspiring entrepreneurs and visionary young individuals ready to challenge conventions, create impactful ventures, and shape the future. It’s an investment in raw talent and audacious ideas, providing the freedom and resources to transform a concept into a thriving reality.

Peter Thiel, known for co-founding PayPal and Palantir Technologies, established the fellowship to support a new generation of founders who believe that certain kinds of learning and innovation are best done by doing, not by sitting in a classroom.

Frequently Asked Questions about the Thiel Fellowship

What is the Thiel Fellowship?

The Thiel Fellowship is a two-year program that provides $200,000 to young innovators and entrepreneurs under the age of 23, empowering them to pursue their bold ideas and build new ventures instead of attending college. It's an entrepreneurial fellowship focusing on real-world impact.

Who is eligible to apply for the Thiel Fellowship?

The fellowship is open to aspiring innovators and entrepreneurs, typically under 23 years old, who are committed to building something new and have decided against or paused traditional higher education paths. We look for individuals with drive, vision, and a tangible project idea. This is an excellent high school entrepreneur grants opportunity.

What benefits does the Thiel Fellowship offer besides the $200,000 grant?

Beyond the $200,000 grant, fellows gain access to a powerful network of mentors, investors, and fellow innovators in Silicon Valley, receive personalized guidance, and are free from the conventional academic pressures and student debt, allowing them to focus entirely on their projects. It's a robust startup mentorship program.

What happens during the two-year fellowship program?

During the two years, fellows work on their projects, guided by mentors from various industries. There isn't a fixed curriculum, but opportunities for workshops, retreats, and networking events are provided. The focus is on independent work, learning by doing, and leveraging the network to overcome challenges.

Is skipping college a permanent decision if I join the Thiel Fellowship?

Skipping college for the fellowship is a personal decision, and it's not necessarily permanent. Many fellows find the experience so transformative that they continue with their ventures. Others may choose to pursue higher education later, but with real-world experience and accomplishments under their belt, they gain immense benefits of thiel fellowship over traditional university paths.

How can I get a grant to start a company without college?

The Thiel Fellowship is specifically designed for this! By applying and being selected, you receive a $200,000 grant to fund your startup, along with mentorship and resources, all without the need for a traditional college degree. We offer one of the premier college alternative programs for budding entrepreneurs.
